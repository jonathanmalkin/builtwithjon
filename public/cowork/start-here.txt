Pick a name first

Pick a name for your assistant, anything you like, such as Margaret or Pam. You
will give your Cowork Project that same name, and your assistant will answer to
it. Wherever the guide says [Name], use the name you picked.


Setup in one path: install, run setup, answer a few questions, done

Before you start, have ready: Claude Desktop installed (claude.com/download), a
Gmail account and Google Calendar, the file personal-assistant-cowork-plugin.zip
(in your Downloads), and a name for your assistant.

The short version:

1. Create a Cowork Project. Open Claude, click the Cowork tab, click Projects >
   New Project, select "Use an existing folder," create or choose any folder on
   your computer, and name the folder and Project with your assistant's name.
2. Install the add-on. In Customize > Personal Plugins, click +, Create Plugin,
   Upload Plugin, and pick personal-assistant-cowork-plugin.zip.
3. Connect Gmail and Google Calendar in Customize > Connectors (skip if already
   connected).
4. Optional: to get your brief texted to you, add the "Read and Send iMessages"
   connector in Customize > Connectors.
5. Open your Project, type "Run the setup skill," and answer its few questions.
   When Claude prompts you to create the Daily Brief artifact and scheduled task,
   click Create.
6. Open Scheduled, select Daily Brief Refresh, click Run Now, select the session
   that starts, and approve all permissions so it can run daily.
7. Open Live Artifacts and click Daily Brief.
8. Optional: set up Dispatch on your phone afterward.

The setup skill does the rest: it interviews you and writes your assistant's
memory (CLAUDE.md, USER.md, now.md, decision-log.md), finds your Gmail and
Calendar tool names, builds your live Daily Brief page, and creates your weekday
morning routine (8:00 AM by default, on your default Claude model). You do not
make any files by hand.

One small thing you do by hand before setup: paste the assistant instructions
block from Start-Here.html into the Project Instructions field, so your assistant
always loads its instructions.

The questions setup asks: your assistant's name (it suggests the Project name)
and, only if you added the iMessage connector, the phone number or email
associated with iMessage. It detects your time zone and uses 8 AM on weekdays
unless you say otherwise.

The add-on is installed once from Customize > Personal Plugins and then used
inside the Project.


Your Daily Brief

After setup you have a live Daily Brief page. Your schedule and any email drafts
awaiting approval refresh every time you open it. Your goals, meeting prep,
to-dos, and waiting items refresh each weekday morning. It is read-only: to clear
a to-do, tell your assistant what you finished and it updates now.md. Email
replies are only ever drafts for you to review and send; nothing is sent for you.


Copy-ready daily prompts

Inside your [Name] project on the computer, you can use short prompts. From
Dispatch on your phone, start with "Use my [Name] project and..." first.

"Run my morning brief for today. Create HTML and plain text outputs. Do not send
email or change calendar events."

"Run the email-triage skill on my last 48 hours of Gmail. Create Gmail drafts
only. Print the drafts here. Do not send anything."

"Prep me for tomorrow's meetings. Create HTML and plain text outputs."

"Capture this update: [type your decision, follow-up, or note about a person]."


Reports

The assistant should create readable outputs:

- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-morning-brief.html
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-morning-brief.txt
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-email-triage.html
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-email-triage.txt
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-meeting-prep-[short-title].html
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-meeting-prep-[short-title].txt

Claude can still use Markdown internally for CLAUDE.md, USER.md, now.md,
decision-log.md, and voice.md. You should not need to open those files during
normal use.


Safety boundary

The assistant can read connected sources, create local notes, draft replies, and
write reports. It does not send email to other people, post publicly, delete
files, purchase anything, or change calendar events without your explicit
approval. The only outbound messages it sends on its own are Gmail drafts (never
sent for you) and, if you turn it on, the morning brief texted to your configured
iMessage phone number or email. iMessage is one-way: the assistant does not read
your texts or take commands from them. Inbound phone requests go through
Dispatch. Telegram is an advanced/custom channel, not the basic setup path.
