Workshop follow-up

Your personal assistant in Claude Cowork

This is the complete guide for setting up and using the assistant in Cowork. Cowork is the main path, Dispatch is the phone path, and human-facing reports come out as HTML and plain text instead of raw Markdown. Workshop Slides are here.

What this gives you

  • 1A Cowork Project that knows your profile, goals, agenda, people, and assistant rules.
  • 2One validated assistant plugin with the workshop skills bundled together.
  • 3A day-to-day operating guide for briefs, email triage, meeting prep, capture, voice, learning, and skill updates.
  • 4Two ways to use it: Cowork on your computer and Dispatch on your phone.

Setup

Build your assistant in Cowork

1

Pick a name for your assistant

Pick a name, anything you like, such as Margaret or Pam. You will use this one name for the folder, the Cowork Project, and the assistant itself, so you can later just say "Use my [Name] project."

Wherever this guide shows [Name], type the name you picked.

2

Make one folder on your computer

This folder is the assistant's home base. Claude reads and writes files here when you work in the Project.

  • On your computer, make a new folder and give it your assistant's name, for example Margaret or Pam.
3

Create the Cowork Project

  1. Open Claude Desktop.
  2. At the top, switch from Chat to Cowork.
  3. Open Projects, click the New Project button, and choose Use an existing folder.
  4. Pick the folder you made in step 2.
  5. Name the Project the same name as your assistant, [Name]. That one name is how you will call it later, like "Use my [Name] project."
  6. Click Create to finish.
4

Paste the assistant instructions

Copy the box below with its Copy button and paste it into the Project's Instructions field. Then replace [Name] with your assistant's name.

You are [Name], my personal assistant inside this Cowork Project.

READ THESE FILES FIRST
- CLAUDE.md - how you should work, my preferences, and the approval rules.
- USER.md - who I am, my goals, and the people who matter.
- workspace/Agenda/now.md - what I am working on now and what I am waiting on.
- workspace/Agenda/decision-log.md - decisions worth remembering.
If these files are missing, run the interview skill to create them.

USE THE ASSISTANT SKILLS
If the slash menu is available, skills may appear with a plugin name like
personal-assistant-cowork:morning-brief. Use the matching skill when I ask:
- interview - first-time setup or refreshing my profile.
- morning-brief - daily brief, catch-up, and "what am I forgetting?"
- email-triage - review my inbox and create Gmail drafts. Draft only. Never send.
- meeting-prep - briefs for upcoming meetings and the people in them.
- capture - when I give you a decision, follow-up, meeting note, or update about a person.
- teach - when I tell you a lasting preference or correct how you work.
- skill-builder - only when I ask you to make or change a skill.

HUMAN-READABLE OUTPUTS
Do not make me open Markdown files for normal reports.
For morning brief, email triage, and meeting prep, create BOTH:
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-[report-name].html
- outputs/YYYY-MM-DD-[report-name].txt
Keep Markdown only for the behind-the-scenes files: CLAUDE.md, USER.md,
workspace/Agenda/now.md, workspace/Agenda/decision-log.md, workspace/Agenda/voice.md.

DISPATCH (PHONE)
When work comes in through Dispatch, treat it like a normal Project request.
If I say "Use my [Name] project...", use these instructions and the skills.
Dispatch is the normal phone path. Do not require iMessage, Telegram, or Claude
Code for normal morning brief, email triage, meeting prep, or capture tasks.

EMAIL AND CALENDAR
Prefer Gmail and Google Calendar.
- You may read all my calendars, including shared ones, for context.
- For email, you may read and search, and create Gmail drafts. You may not send email.
- Do not create, move, or delete any calendar event without my approval of the
  exact change. If you want to propose time blocks, draft them in chat for me to add.
- If I am on a Microsoft 365 work account, treat its mail and calendar as read-only context.

APPROVAL BOUNDARY
You may read, summarize, draft, and create files in this Project.
You must NOT send email to other people, post anywhere public, delete my data,
buy anything, or change a calendar event without my approval of the exact action.
I can approve a draft or a change, but silence is never approval.

That is all for this step. You do not make any other files by hand. The interview skill makes your starter files in step 7.

5

Install the assistant plugin

  1. In Claude, open Customize.
  2. Open Plugins.
  3. Choose the upload option and select personal-assistant-cowork-plugin.zip.
  4. Open the plugin details and confirm the skills appear.
  5. In a task, type / or click + to see the installed skills.

Simple version: the Project is the assistant's room. The plugin is the toolbox Claude uses in that room. You install it once and it works in every Project.

6

Connect one Gmail and one Google Calendar

  1. In Claude, open Customize.
  2. Open Connectors.
  3. Connect the Gmail account you want the assistant to read for triage and briefs.
  4. Connect the Google Calendar on that same account, for meetings and availability.

The assistant reads your inbox and calendars for context. It drafts email only, and it never sends mail or changes a calendar event without your approval.

7

Run the one setup prompt

Open your [Name] Project and paste this.

Run the interview skill.

The interview asks you a few questions and makes your starter files for you. It already sees that this Project is called [Name], so it will use that as your assistant's name and only check with you once. When it finishes, paste this quick check:

Check that my Gmail and my Google Calendar are connected. Do not send any email and do not change any calendar event. Just tell me what is connected.

8

Set up Dispatch for your phone

  1. Install or update the Claude app on your phone.
  2. Keep Claude Desktop open and your computer awake when you want phone tasks to run.
  3. Open Dispatch from the phone app.
  4. Paste this check:

Use my [Name] project and run a quick connection check. Confirm you can use the [Name] project, the assistant skills, Gmail, and Google Calendar. Do not send email and do not change any calendar event.

After this works, you can use Dispatch from your phone for quick requests like briefs, meeting prep, and capture notes.

9

Schedule the weekday morning brief

Do this last, after steps 1 to 8 all work.

  1. In Claude Desktop, click Scheduled in the left sidebar.
  2. Click + New task in the top right.
  3. Give it a task name, such as Morning brief.
  4. Set the frequency to on weekdays and pick a time in the morning.
  5. For Which folder Claude should work in, choose your [Name] folder.
  6. In the prompt field, paste the prompt below, then save the task.

Run the morning-brief skill. Create HTML and plain text outputs. When you finish, leave this note: Done: Morning Brief. Files: [HTML path], [text path]. Data freshness: [status]. Needs review: [anything I should approve]. Nothing was sent or changed.

Save the task. Every scheduled run should leave a short note, name the files it made, and say if anything needs your approval.

10

Turn it on for every morning

One last step, so the brief can run on its own without stopping to ask you each day:

  1. Open the task you just made and click Run now.
  2. When Claude asks for permission to use your connectors and skills, choose the option to allow it for all future runs, not just this once.

If you only allow it once, the scheduled run will stop and wait for you each day. Allowing all future runs lets it finish on its own and send you the result.

Daily use

Using your assistant day to day

There are two places you will use it: on your computer in Cowork, and on your phone with Dispatch. Wherever you see [Name], use your assistant's name.

Claude Cowork Sample Prompts

Open your [Name] Project. Inside it, the folder, instructions, memory, plugin, and skills are already in scope, so you can talk plainly. You do not need to say the name when you are already inside the Project.

  • 1Say what you want as an outcome. The assistant picks the right skill.
  • 2Ask for HTML and text outputs when you want a report you can read or share.
  • 3To make something stick, say "capture this" or "remember this as a preference."
  • 4The assistant drafts; you approve, send, or change. It never sends mail or edits your calendar on its own.

Sample prompts

Run my morning brief for today. Create HTML and plain text outputs. Do not send email or change calendar events.

Run the email-triage skill on my last 48 hours of Gmail. Create Gmail drafts only. Print the drafts here. Do not send anything.

Prep me for tomorrow's meetings. Create HTML and plain text outputs.

Capture this update: [type your decision, follow-up, or note about a person].

Mobile Dispatch Sample Prompts

Use Dispatch when you are away from your desk or want a task to run while you do something else. Your computer needs to be awake with Claude Desktop open. Because you are not inside the Project on your phone, start every message by naming your assistant, like "Use my [Name] project."

  • 1Name the assistant, then the outcome, then anything that should not happen without approval.
  • 2When it finishes, read the completion note first. It says what files were made and whether anything needs your review.

Sample prompts

Use my [Name] project and prep me for tomorrow's meetings. Create HTML and plain text outputs. Do not send email or change calendar events.

Use my [Name] project and run my morning brief for today. Reply here with the plain text version and save the HTML output.

Use my [Name] project and capture this update: [decision, follow-up, or note about a person].

Skills

What each assistant skill does

These example prompts are written for when you are already inside your Project on your computer, so they do not repeat the name. From your phone in Dispatch, start with "Use my [Name] project and..." first.

interview

Sets up or refreshes the assistant profile files. Use it when you are starting fresh, adding missing context, or changing the assistant's operating rules.

Run the interview skill to refresh my assistant profile. Ask only for missing or stale information.

morning-brief

Reads agenda, profile, calendar, and inbox signals. Updates now.md, then writes a morning brief as HTML and text.

See an example

Run the morning-brief skill for today. Create HTML and plain text outputs. Do not send email or change calendar events.

email-triage

Reviews recent Gmail, classifies threads, creates Gmail drafts when available, prints drafts in chat, and writes HTML/TXT reports. Nothing is sent.

See an example

Run the email-triage skill on my last 48 hours of Gmail. Create Gmail drafts only. Print all draft text here. Nothing should be sent.

meeting-prep

Finds upcoming meetings, identifies attendees, searches available context, and writes meeting prep reports plus contact briefs.

See an example

Run the meeting-prep skill for tomorrow's meetings. Create HTML and plain text outputs.

capture

Records decisions, follow-ups, delegated items, completed items, meeting notes, and contact updates in the local agenda files.

Capture this decision: [what changed]. Update the relevant agenda files and do not take any external action.

teach

Stores lasting preferences in CLAUDE.md or durable user facts in USER.md, with explicit approval.

Remember this as a lasting preference: I prefer short direct replies by default. Show me the proposed update before saving it.

skill-builder

Creates or updates assistant skills when you want a repeatable workflow. It should only run when you ask for a skill change.

Make this a reusable skill for my weekly board update. Draft the skill first and show me what will change before saving it.

deep-voice-profile

Runs the long-form voice profile workflow and writes the deeper workspace/Agenda/voice.md used by writing and email workflows.

Run the deep-voice-profile skill. Save the result to workspace/Agenda/voice.md and ask before overwriting useful existing voice context.

Technical information

Advanced details and official documentation

This section is for instructors, admins, and power users. Participants can use the setup, daily-use, and skills sections above without reading this.

Multiple Google accounts

Mail

  1. Choose one Google account as the assistant's home base.
  2. Forward important mail from other accounts into the home-base Gmail account.
  3. Add labels such as Work Account, Personal Account, or Client Account so email triage can preserve source context.
  4. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and treat labeled forwarded mail as separate source accounts during email triage. Create Gmail drafts only when available. Nothing should be sent."

Calendar

  1. Share the other Google calendars into the home-base Google account with read visibility.
  2. Use all shared calendars as read-only context for availability and meeting prep.
  3. The assistant does not change any calendar event without your approval of the exact change.
  4. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and treat all my shared calendars as read-only context for availability and meeting prep. Do not create, update, or delete any calendar event without my approval of the exact change."

Microsoft Office 365 / Microsoft 365

Use this for work accounts tied to a Microsoft Entra tenant. Personal Outlook and Hotmail accounts do not use this connector.

Mail

  1. Ask a Microsoft Entra Global Administrator to grant tenant consent for the Microsoft 365 connector.
  2. After consent, connect Microsoft 365 from Claude Customize, then Connectors.
  3. Treat Outlook mail as read/search context for summaries, meeting prep, and triage. Do not promise sending or draft creation through Microsoft 365 unless the current connector exposes that safely.
  4. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and check whether Microsoft 365 Outlook context is available. Use it for read-only context only. Do not send email or modify mailbox state."

Calendar

  1. Use Microsoft 365 calendar as read/search context when the connector is available.
  2. Have Claude draft any proposed event details in chat for you to add. Do not rely on Microsoft 365 for calendar writes; the connector is read-only.
  3. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and use Microsoft 365 calendar as read-only context for meeting prep and availability. Draft any proposed calendar changes in chat for my approval."

Personal Outlook and Hotmail

Personal Microsoft accounts such as Outlook.com and Hotmail do not work with the Microsoft 365 connector that requires a Microsoft Entra tenant.

Mail

  1. Forward important personal Outlook or Hotmail messages into the connected Gmail account.
  2. Label the forwarded messages as Outlook or Hotmail.
  3. Use Gmail email triage on the labeled forwarded mail.
  4. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and include messages labeled Outlook or Hotmail when triaging email. Treat them as forwarded messages from my personal Microsoft account. Create Gmail drafts only when available. Nothing should be sent."

Calendar

  1. Subscribe to or share the Outlook calendar into the connected Google Calendar account when possible.
  2. Use the imported Outlook calendar as read-only context.
  3. The assistant does not change any calendar event without your approval of the exact change.
  4. In your [Name] project, paste: "Use my [Name] project and treat my imported Outlook calendar as read-only context for availability and meeting prep. Do not create, update, or delete any calendar event without my approval."

Communication options: Slack, iMessage, Telegram

The two main ways to reach the assistant, covered in the steps above, are the Cowork Project on the computer and Dispatch on the phone. The options below are advanced and optional. Most people should just use Dispatch.

  • Cowork Project (recommended): best for setup, files, reports, and repeatable workflows. Open your [Name] Project and work there first. See Cowork Projects docs.
  • Dispatch (recommended): best for two-way mobile use. Ask from Claude mobile, name your [Name] project, and Claude reports the outcome back in Dispatch. See Dispatch docs.
  • Slack (optional): use Claude in Slack for a team conversation or Slack DM. If your Slack session cannot reach the Project files, switch to Dispatch.
  • iMessage or Telegram (advanced): use Claude Code channels only when Dispatch is not enough and you specifically need Messages or a Telegram bot bridged into a running local Claude Code session.

iMessage and Telegram are advanced. They are a research-preview feature, they need the Claude Code app (version 2.1.80 or later) with Bun installed, your computer must stay awake with the session running, and on Team or Enterprise plans an admin has to turn channels on first.
