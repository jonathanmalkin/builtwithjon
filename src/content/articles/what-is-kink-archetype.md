You've probably been asked whether you're dominant or submissive. Maybe you answered confidently. Maybe you gave a hedged "it depends." Maybe the question felt like it missed something important about how you actually think about desire.

The dom/sub axis is real. It's just not the whole picture.

A kink archetype captures something broader: the pattern of what draws you in. Not just where you sit on a single scale, but which dimensions of erotic experience actually light up for you and how they combine. Two people can both identify as submissive and want completely different things in a scene. One craves restraint and the quiet intensity that comes with it. The other wants to be seen, admired, performed for. The label "submissive" covers both of them, but it doesn't explain either of them.

That's what the archetype framework tries to fix.

## Where the 9 archetypes come from

The [Kink Archetype Quiz](https://quiz.9archetypes.app) measures 8 independent dimensions of kink: power dynamics, service and devotion, care and nurturing, sensation, restraint, pain and intensity, exhibition and voyeurism, and primal instinct. Your scores across all 8 produce a profile, and that profile maps to one of 9 archetypes.

The archetypes aren't categories you slot yourself into. They're patterns that emerged from the structure of the dimensions themselves. Most people land in one clearly; some find themselves genuinely split between two.

Here's what each one means.

## The 9 Kink Archetypes

### [Power Player](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/power-player)

The Power Player is drawn to the exchange of authority. Control and surrender are both erotic, depending on the direction you tend. For some Power Players that means holding the reins — directing, deciding, watching someone trust them with that. For others it means letting go completely, handing over decisions, feeling held by structure.

What defines this archetype isn't dominance or submission specifically. It's that the power dynamic itself is the thing. The frame matters as much as what happens inside it.

### [Sensation Seeker](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/sensation-seeker)

Sensation Seekers are drawn to the full range of physical experience. Temperature, texture, pressure, impact, softness. Ice cubes and wax. Feathers and floggers. The body as something to be explored deliberately.

This isn't about intensity per se — it's about attention. Sensation Seekers tend to be highly attuned to physical experience and curious about what different inputs do. The draw is exploratory and often sensory-rich in everyday life too.

### [Care Curator](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/care-curator)

Care Curators are organized around holding space. Making someone feel safe, seen, protected. The erotic charge here runs through nurture and trust — the deep satisfaction of being the person someone can fully relax into, or being that person yourself.

This archetype shows up in caregiving dynamics (Daddy/Mommy, little, caregiver/dependent), but it's not limited to them. Anywhere the emotional container is as important as what happens inside it, you'll find Care Curator energy.

### [Pain Enthusiast](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/pain-enthusiast)

Consensual pain as a vehicle for intensity, altered states, and connection. That's the Pain Enthusiast's domain. The endorphin cascade, the mental clarity that sometimes follows a challenging scene, the intimacy of giving or receiving something that requires real trust.

This archetype often gets reduced to "masochist" or "sadist," but those labels are narrower than what's actually happening. Pain Enthusiasts are typically interested in the psychological and physical experience together, not just the sensation in isolation.

### [Bondage Lover](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/bondage-lover)

Restraint, containment, rope art, the feeling of being held still or holding someone still. Bondage Lovers are drawn to the specific experience of physical restriction. For some that's rope: the aesthetics, the skill, the ritual of it. For others it's cuffs, tape, furniture, or even just a firm grip.

What the archetype captures is that the restriction itself is the point. Not a means to something else — the experience of being unable to move, or making someone unable to move, is inherently significant.

### [Exhibition Enthusiast](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/exhibition-enthusiast)

Being seen. Watching. The electricity of visibility and the intimacy that can exist between performer and observer. Exhibition Enthusiasts are drawn to the relational dimension of being perceived — the charge that comes from knowing someone is watching, or from watching with full attention.

This shows up in exhibitionism and voyeurism, obviously, but also in play that involves an audience, in feeling seen during intimacy even in private, in the performance dimension of dominance or submission.

### [Service Specialist](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/service-specialist)

Devotion through action. Service Specialists find deep satisfaction in doing things for people they've chosen to serve — not because they have to, but because the act of service itself carries meaning. Protocol, ritual, specific tasks done well: these are how connection happens for this archetype.

It's worth being clear that this isn't about compliance. Service Specialists typically have strong opinions about how things should be done. The service is chosen and intentional, which is exactly what makes it meaningful.

### [Primal Player](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/primal-player)

Raw, body-led, instinctual. Primal Players are drawn to the animal layer of experience — wrestling, chase, the kind of play that doesn't follow a script because the point is to drop the script. Connection through physical contest or surrender, where the body leads and the thinking mind follows.

This archetype often resonates with people who find heavily structured scenes constraining. The draw is toward something more immediate, more spontaneous, more physical.

### [Explorer](https://quiz.9archetypes.app/archetypes/explorer)

Curiosity across the board. Explorers score meaningfully across multiple dimensions without a single dominant pattern — and that's the pattern. The draw is toward breadth, toward trying things, toward not having settled into a fixed identity around kink.

Explorer isn't a placeholder for "undecided." It's a genuine archetype. Explorers tend to be highly adaptive partners, comfortable in a range of scenes, interested in what their partner wants as much as what they want themselves.

## How archetypes actually work

A few things worth knowing before you take the quiz.

The archetypes aren't exclusive. Your result reflects your highest pattern, but you're scored across all 8 dimensions. If you're a Bondage Lover with high sensation scores, that shows up in your full profile even if the archetype label is singular.

They're not fixed. What draws you in at 25 might be different at 40. Your archetype can shift. The quiz is a snapshot, not a diagnosis.

They're not rankings. No archetype is more sophisticated or more "advanced" than another. They're just different patterns of what's interesting.

And they're not prescriptive. Knowing your archetype doesn't tell you what to do. It gives you language for what you already know about yourself.
