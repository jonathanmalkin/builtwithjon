## Article 2: The Missed-Call Math
`https://builtwithjon.com/articles/missed-call-math-home-services/`

### Draft A — the invisible leak

> A lost bid at least leaves a dead proposal in your files.
>
> A missed call leaves nothing. No record, no name, no feedback. The job goes to whoever answered, and your month just looks a little slow.
>
> 30 missed calls a month. A third were real jobs. $450 average ticket.
>
> $54K a year, walking to a competitor — not because your work is worse, but because nobody picked up.
>
> Pull your call log before you believe me. Then read this: [link]

### Draft B — the 20-line fix

> The highest-leverage automation in home services is about 20 lines of workflow:
>
> Every missed call gets an instant text back. "Got your call — dealing with something urgent? Reply here and we'll get you scheduled."
>
> The homeowner's next-name-on-the-list reflex stops the moment someone responds. A text counts.
>
> What the text should NOT do: quote, diagnose, or promise arrival times. Those stay human.
>
> The full missed-call math for a small shop, plus the invoice-chase twin nobody talks about: [link]
