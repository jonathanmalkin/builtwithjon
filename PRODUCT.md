# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners, founders, CEOs, COOs, and practical operators at small and lower-mid-size businesses, generally around $10M-$50M in revenue plus smaller owner-operated businesses with real operational pain and authority to act. They want less busywork, faster follow-up, cleaner handoffs, better visibility, and a practical first AI win without an enterprise transformation project.

## Product Purpose

Built with Jon presents Jonathan Malkin as an experienced hands-on partner who improves business operations one workflow at a time. The precise category and pitch are under active review; the site should not prematurely lock Jonathan into “fractional AI operator” as the primary identity. Its immediate job is to help a qualified visitor recognize operational leaks, complete the free AI workflow scorecard, and understand the path from an assumption-based score to a measured review, owned implementation, or hands-on workshop.

## Brand Personality

Practical, credible, and human. The experience should feel like a clear working session with an experienced operator: technically capable without tool hype, warm without becoming cute, and confident without corporate fog.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic blue-gradient SaaS pages, dark hacker-terminal styling, tool-stack flexing, AI-agency peer language, glossy enterprise-consulting abstraction, literal coffee-shop theming, and creator-growth funnels aimed at prompt collectors.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the business leak; AI is the hook and operations is the job.
- Make the scorecard the unmistakable front door and preserve a clear path to the measured review.
- Show practical evidence and operating judgment before technical sophistication.
- Keep every first step concrete, low-risk, and understandable to a non-technical decision-maker.
- Preserve the site's fast, low-JavaScript, readable foundation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG 2.2 AA as the practical baseline. Preserve keyboard access, visible focus, semantic structure, useful alternative text, sufficient contrast, readable type, reduced-motion support where motion exists, and responsive behavior. Accessibility supports usability and reach but is not the site's primary positioning theme.
